Radioactivity

Radioactivity is the spontaneous emission of radiation by unstable atomic nuclei. This process transforms an unstable nucleus into a more stable one by releasing energy in the form of particles or electromagnetic waves.

Types of Radioactive Decay

  • Alpha (α) decay: Emission of an alpha particle (2 protons and 2 neutrons).

  • Beta (β) decay: Emission of a beta particle (electron or positron).

  • Gamma (γ) decay: Emission of high-energy electromagnetic radiation.

Properties of Radiation

  • Alpha particles have low penetration power and can be stopped by paper or skin.

  • Beta particles have moderate penetration and can be stopped by aluminum sheets.

  • Gamma rays have high penetration and require thick lead or concrete to block.

Applications

  • Medical treatment, such as cancer radiotherapy.

  • Radioactive dating to determine the age of fossils and rocks.

  • Power generation in nuclear reactors.

  • Industrial applications, including tracing and sterilization.

Safety Measures

  • Use of shielding to block radiation.

  • Maintaining a safe distance from radioactive sources.

  • Minimizing exposure time.

  • Wearing protective clothing and equipment.
