Introduction

Carboxylic acids are organic compounds characterized by the presence of a carboxyl group (-COOH). They are widely distributed in nature and have significant applications in various industries.

Structure and Nomenclature

  • The general formula for carboxylic acids is R-COOH, where R represents an alkyl or aryl group.

  • In IUPAC nomenclature, carboxylic acids are named by replacing the "-e" of the parent alkane with "-oic acid" (e.g., methane → methanoic acid).

  • Common names are also used, such as acetic acid for ethanoic acid.

Physical Properties

  • Carboxylic acids are polar compounds due to the electronegativity of the oxygen atoms.

  • They exhibit hydrogen bonding, leading to higher boiling points compared to other organic compounds of similar molecular weight.

  • Lower molecular weight acids are soluble in water, while higher molecular weight acids are less soluble.

Chemical Properties

  • Carboxylic acids are weak acids and can donate a proton (H+) to form a carboxylate anion (RCOO-).

  • They react with alcohols to form esters in a reaction known as esterification.

  • They can undergo reduction to form primary alcohols.

  • Carboxylic acids can also react with bases to form salts and water.

Applications

  • Used in the production of soaps and detergents.

  • Serve as intermediates in the synthesis of pharmaceuticals and agrochemicals.

  • Found in food additives and preservatives.

  • Utilized in the manufacture of polymers and plastics.

Note: Carboxylic acids play a crucial role in various biochemical processes and industrial applications.
