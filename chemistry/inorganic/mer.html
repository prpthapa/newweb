Class 12 chemistry Notes — Mercury
1 / 3

Introduction

  • Symbol: Hg

  • Atomic Number: 80

  • Appearance: Silvery-white liquid metal at room temperature

  • Occurrence: Found in nature as cinnabar (HgS); extracted via roasting to obtain mercury vapor, which is then condensed.

Physical Properties

  • State: Liquid at room temperature

  • Density: 13.6 g/cm³

  • Boiling Point: 356.7°C

  • Freezing Point: −38.83°C

  • Electrical Conductivity: Good conductor of electricity

  • Thermal Conductivity: High thermal conductivity

Chemical Properties

  • Oxidation States: +1 (mercurous) and +2 (mercuric)

  • Reactivity: Reacts with halogens to form mercuric halides (e.g., HgCl₂)

  • Formation of Mercury(I) Compounds: Involves two mercury atoms, resulting in compounds like Hg₂Cl₂ (calomel)

Uses

  • Industrial Applications:

    • Manufacture of caustic soda

    • Extraction of gold and silver

    • Production of mercury vapor lamps

    • Silvering of mirrors

    • Dental amalgams

  • Scientific Instruments:

    • Thermometers

    • Barometers

    • Manometers

Toxicity

  • Mercuric Compounds: Highly toxic; can cause damage to kidneys and nervous system

  • Mercurous Compounds: Less toxic but still hazardous

  • Elemental Mercury: Toxic when inhaled as vapor; can lead to respiratory and neurological issues

Safety Measures

  • Handling: Use gloves and proper ventilation when working with mercury and its compounds

  • Spill Management: Clean up spills immediately using proper procedures to prevent exposure
